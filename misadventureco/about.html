Our Story

We started with day-old bread. And a hunch.

// Estd. Vista, CA
18×
awards.
From surplus baked goods.

Here's the quick version. A few years ago, we looked at how much perfectly good bread was getting thrown out every single day by bakeries across California — tens of thousands of pounds — and we wondered if it could be made into something. Turns out it could. Turns out it could be made into really good vodka.

So we built a distillery in Vista. We figured out how to ferment day-old bread, how to distill it, how to make the vodka taste clean and soft and a little bit bright. It took us longer than we planned. It was a genuine misadventure. That's where the name comes from.

Eighteen awards later, we still operate the same way. Every bottle of Misadventure Vodka starts as surplus baked goods that would have been landfilled. We're not putting that on the label for marketing points — it's the entire reason we exist.

The tasting room came next. We wanted somewhere the story could be told in person, where you could sip the vodka neat, try cocktails with Blood Runs Cold and Lychee Chi Chi and Crime of Passion, and hang out in North County for an afternoon. So we built that too. Cozy, whimsical, warm. Exactly the way we wanted it.

Vista deserves more attention than it gets. We're doing our part. Come find us.

— The Misadventure Team
