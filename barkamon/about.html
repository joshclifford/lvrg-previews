Our Story

We built the bar we wanted to drink at.

We grew up on the kind of bars that don't exist anymore. The kind where the bartender knows your drink before you sit down, where the lighting is low enough that conversation happens honestly, where every detail — the glass, the ice, the way a drink lands on the coaster — was chosen by a human who cares.

So we built one. In East Village, behind an unmarked door at the back of Asa Cafe & Bakery, Bar Kamon opened as a love letter to Japan's Taisho Roman era — that brief, beautiful window between East and West when craftsmen in Tokyo built rooms of wood and crystal and called them home.

Our team — six bartenders with between six and fifteen years behind the stick — has spent years studying this stuff. We clarify our own cocktails. We age our own infusions. We source Hojicha from a single family in Kyoto. We will happily spend twelve minutes building your drink if that's what the drink needs.

San Diego deserved somewhere quieter. Something patient. A room that feels like it has always been here.

— The Bar Kamon Team
