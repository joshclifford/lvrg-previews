Our Story

A table built for everyone, on the right corner of Hillcrest.

We built insideOUT because San Diego deserved a restaurant that felt like the city itself — warm, inclusive, open-air, just a little bit joyful. Hillcrest was the only neighborhood that made sense. It's the most welcoming square mile in the county, and we wanted to give it a dining room that matched.

From day one, the architecture was non-negotiable. An open-air atrium. A water feature running through the center of the room. Custom tables handcrafted by friends in our community. The room had to feel like a garden you found by accident — somewhere you could linger for three hours and forget what time it was.

Then we handed the kitchen to Executive Chef Johnny Duran. Johnny works California ingredients through a Mediterranean lens — bright, seasonal, never showy. He cooks for the neighborhood first and the food critics second, which is why the regulars keep coming back.

We're proud to be San Diego's premier LGBTQ-owned restaurant. We're prouder that the line between "community" and "customer" disappears the moment you walk in. First date, twentieth anniversary, corporate dinner for 30 — it's the same welcome. That was always the point.

— The insideOUT Team
